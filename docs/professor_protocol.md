# Professor Protocol

## Overview

Professor Protocol is a conceptual framework describing collaborative reasoning and architectural co-design between human strategic intent and AI-assisted structuring systems.

The protocol defines a distributed interaction model where:
- humans provide direction, intent, contextual judgment, and conceptual meaning,
- while AI systems assist in structuring, expansion, formalization, and adaptive reasoning.

The objective of the protocol is not to replace human cognition, but to amplify architectural thinking, system modeling, and conceptual development.

## Core Principles

- Architecture before implementation.
- Distributed reasoning over centralized control.
- Continuous fixation of evolving concepts.
- Adaptive collaboration between human and AI layers.
- Stability through structured interaction and iterative refinement.

## Human Role

The human layer is responsible for:
- defining strategic direction,
- evaluating meaning,
- preserving conceptual continuity,
- and making final architectural decisions.

## AI Role

AI-assisted systems contribute through:
- concept structuring,
- pattern expansion,
- adaptive formalization,
- scenario generation,
- and reasoning support.

The AI layer operates as a collaborative reasoning environment rather than an autonomous decision authority.

## Interaction Cycle

The protocol operates through iterative cycles:

Intent → Discussion → Structuring → Validation → Fixation → System Evolution

## Architectural Goal

The objective of the protocol is to enable reproducible human-AI collaborative architecture development for distributed adaptive systems.

## Relation to ABSP

Professor Protocol acts as a meta-architectural reasoning layer supporting the development and evolution of ABSP-based systems.
